Miles Davis - Kind of Blue
All Blues
"All Blues" is a 6/8 12-measure blues form that produces its mood through only a few modal changes and Miles Davis' free melodic conception.

Intro/Vamp 0:00 Bass, drums, and piano enter together on the 6/8 groove.  Bill Evans plays a light two-handed trill on the piano.  Jimmy Cobb plays with brushes.  Paul Chambers plays the repeating bass line that will continue for most of the tune.
  0:10 Saxophones enter with a harmonized line that repeats every measure.
Head 0:20 Miles enters with the melody. The form is a 12 bar blues with a modal twist: instead of the IV chord in the 5th bar, the bass stays on the tonic, creating a minor chord.  The bass line only is released on the third phrase of the blues, when it switches to two notes per bar.
Vamp 0:52 Four bars, same as above.
Head 1:03 The melody is repeated with slight variations from Miles.
Vamp 1:34 The high hat is louder, and more sustained leading to the solo section.
Trumpet Solo 1:44 Jimmy Cobb switches to sticks on the cymbal and Miles removes the mute for his solo. 
  2:16 Cobb converses with Miles by commenting on the snare drum during the rests in the trumpet lines.
  2:47 Third chorus, Miles rides on the tonic, leaving plenty of space for Evans and Cobb to respond.
  3:19 Fourth chorus, Miles moves to a higher exclamatory phrase.  Evan's responds with dramatic chords.

Vamp 10:50 The saxophones, piano, drums (still with sticks), and bass play very softly.  It seem like the music is about to fade out when:
Head 11:01 Miles plays a rhythmic motive in what sounds like a coda, but on the 5th bar the harmony shifts as in the head and Miles returns to the melody on the third phrase.  The tune fades out in the last two bars of the form.  This is the only example on the recording of playing the melody of the tune three times at the end.
