thetext=<HTML><BODY><b>The Hut on Hen's Legs (Baba-Yaga)</b>
Baba Yaga is a witch who lives in a hut supported on four chicken-feet and eats human bones which she grinds up with a mortar and pestle; she also uses the mortar to fly through the air. Hartmann had designed <a href="asfunction:showImage,8"><font color="#FF0000"><u>a clock in the form of Baba Yaga's hut</u></font></a>; this object, however Slavic-Victorian it may appear to modern eyes, was declared by the catalogue of the memorial exhibition to be in the Russian style of the fourteenth century. Mussorgsky's music really deals, of course, with Baba Yaga and her ride through the air rather than with the clock.
