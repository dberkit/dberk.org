thetext=<HTML><BODY><b>Limoges, the Market Place (The Big News)</b>
Hartmann did more than 150 watercolors in Limoges, most of them dealing with the celebrated cathedral of that French city, but some dealing with its picturesque types. <a href="asfunction:showImage,7"><font color="#FF0000"><u>This image</u></font></a> represents a woman (said to have been 112 years old!) praying in the cathedral; just such costumes as hers must have been worn by the market women chattering over their pushcarts in the painting immortalized by this movement.
On the margins of the original manuscript Mussorgsky made two attempts to suggest in prose the conversation set forth in the music. He later scratched these out, but they provide an admirable guide to the interpretation of the movement. The first paragraph reads as follows:
<blockquote>"The great news: Monsieur Pimpant de Panta Pantaleon has just recovered his cow, The Fugitive. 'Yes, Maam, that was yesterday. -- No, Maam, it was day before yesterday. -- Oh well, Maam, the beast roamed all over the neighborhood. -- Oh, no, Maam, the beast never got loose at all.' Etc."
Dissatisfied with this, Mussorgsky rewrote it in this wise:
"The great news: Monsieur de Puissangeout has just recovered his cow, The Fugitive. But the good gossips of Limoges are not in complete accord on this subject because Mme. de Remboursac has just acquired a fine new set of false teeth while Monsieur de Panta-Pantaleon's nose, which is in his way, remains the color of a peony."
</blockquote>
