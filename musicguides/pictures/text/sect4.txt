During the late 1860's Hartmann traveled in Western Europe on a fellowship from the Imperial Academy of Fine Arts, studying monuments of architecture and sending back paintings and drawings of them as evidence of his diligence in this assignment. He often adorned his architectural draftings with human figures to indicate their scale; these figures are frequently in appropriate costume and engaged in appropriate activities. In painting an old castle in Italy, Hartmann suggested the size of the building with the figure of a troubadour singing to the accompaniment of a lute, and this is the reason for the sustained, moonlit, serenade-like character of Mussorgsky's movement. (<a href="asfunction:showImage,2"><font color="#FF0000"><u>This image</u></font></a> of the tower of the Cathedral of Perigueux rising above modern buildings, is Hartmann's only extant painting of this kind.)
