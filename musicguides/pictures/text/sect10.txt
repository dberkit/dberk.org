thetext=<HTML><BODY><b>Two Polish Jews, One Rich, the Other Poor</b>
This movement is usually called <i>Samuel Goldenberg and Schmuyle</i>, but that title is a late addition and has no authority in Mussorgsky's manuscript. The music describes one of Hartmann's Sandomir drawings and was in Mussorgsky's own collection, from which it has since disappeared. We possess, however, two of Hartmann's Sandomir pictures which are not far distant from the lost drawings. <a href="asfunction:showImage,5"><font color="#FF0000"><u>This image</u></font></a> is a Polish Jew who, if not rich, is certainly not poverty-stricken, while <a href="asfunction:showImage,6"><font color="#FF0000"><u>the other</u></font></a> is clearly dirt-poor. The former is a noble head and the latter is tragic; Mussorgsky's picture was a caricature, and this feeling carries over into the music, with its pompous pronouncements of the rich man and the wheedling, nervous, fast-talking triplets of the beggar.
